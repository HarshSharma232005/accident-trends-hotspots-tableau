# Road Accident Analytics & Risk Profiling Dashboard (Tableau)

An end-to-end data visualization project using **Tableau** to analyze comprehensive road accident data. This project transforms raw traffic incident records into an interactive, multi-layered dashboard suite designed to help urban planners, law enforcement, and policy makers identify high-risk zones, understand driver risk profiles, and mitigate future traffic fatalities.

---

##  Dashboards Overview

The project is split into four dedicated operational dashboards, each focusing on a unique dimension of traffic safety:

### 1. Accident Trends & Hotspots Dashboard
* **Core Focus:** High-level executive KPIs and geographic density mapping.
* **Key Insights:** Monitors a total of **15,198 casualties** involving **8,988 vehicles**. Features a comprehensive **Statewise Road Accident Density Map** highlighting high-density regions like Haryana and Madhya Pradesh, along with regional severity distributions (e.g., Dwarka, Lucknow, Surat).

### 2. Driver Behavior & Risk Profile Dashboard
* **Core Focus:** Human factors, licensing, demographics, and temporal behavior patterns.
* **Key Insights:** cross-tabulates vehicle types against accident locations (Bridges, Curves, Intersections). It uncovers alarming trends showing that accidents remain high across all license categories (**Valid, Expired, and None**), and tracks day-wise fatality indexes alongside forecasted casualties.

### 3. Road, Weather & Environmental Impact Dashboard
* **Core Focus:** Outer environmental and infrastructural hazards.
* **Key Insights:** Uses an **Accident Risk Meter (Tree Map)** and speed correlation matrices to map how weather (Stormy, Hazy, Foggy, Rainy) combined with road types (Village, Urban, National Highways) scales up accident severity scores.

### 4. Macro-Level Accident Overview
* **Core Focus:** Multi-year aggregate comparisons (2021 vs. 2022).
* **Key Insights:** Evaluates a massive footprint of **307.97K total accidents** and **418K casualties**, highlighting that the vast majority of incidents occur on **Dry** road surface conditions and peak significantly on **Fridays and Wednesdays**.

---

##  Tech Stack & Tools Used
* **Data Visualization:** Tableau Desktop
* **Mapping & Geocoding:** Mapbox & OpenStreetMap integration
* **Data Layouts Used:** Cross-tabs, Tree Maps, Word Clouds, Stacked Bar Charts, Line/Trend Forecasting, and Bubble Correlation Plots.

---

##  Key Insights & Takeaways
* **The Intersection Hazard:** Intersections and straight roads represent major accident hotspots across almost all vehicle classes, specifically buses and two-wheelers.
* **The Dry Road Paradox:** A significant bulk of total accidents (over 200K) happen under **Dry road surface conditions**, suggesting driver negligence or speeding often outweighs weather hazards.
* **Infrastructure Sensitivity:** Village roads during stormy or hazy conditions exhibit an exponentially higher risk score compared to well-lit or managed urban alternatives.
